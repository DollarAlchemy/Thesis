Rupert Sheldrake

British biologist and author, Rupert Sheldrake is known for his controversial theory of morphic resonance, suggesting that memory and behavior patterns are stored in a collective field shared by all organisms.

Key Works

  • Morphic Resonance (1981) - Proposes that nature is guided by a collective memory rather than fixed laws.
  • Science Set Free (2012) - Challenges conventional scientific beliefs and encourages open-minded inquiry.

Selected Excerpt

"Morphic fields underlie the organization of systems and provide a basis for telepathy-type interconnections between organisms. These fields are built up through repetition, which acts as a kind of memory. They challenge the mechanistic worldview that has dominated science for centuries."
